Developing an Effective
Costumer Management Web Admin

ClientElectium
Timeline5 Weeks
ToolsFigma, Angular and Bootstrap
RoleUX/UI Designer and UI Web Developer

Overview

Electium is a funeral company located in Spain that embarked on a process of updating and modernizing its services. With the goal of improving their services and communication with their clients, they were seeking a Web Admin to develop a mobile application. These solutions would allow them to keep records of their clients, send important notifications, and provide a blog section for their users. The challenge was to develop a simple and effective Web Admin that addressed all their needs, enabling them to efficiently manage their clients.

Research

The client required an Web Admin with three key sections: users, notifications, and blogs, to manage both their users and the application effectively.

Findings:

  • I observed that users of the Web Admin had limited familiarity with technology, highlighting the importance of simplifying the interface.
  • It was crucial to develop a design with a limited number of options and clearly highlight the available actions so that users could easily identify them.

Defining the Solution

Approached the design of Electium with a strategic focus, taking the following actions:
  • I research and analyze references and web applications with similar functionalities.
  • I opte for a simple design, removing unnecessary animations, and focusing on providing what the client needed without excessive visual distractions.
  • I use explicit icons and clear modals with well-designed inputs to minimize user errors.
  • I maintain a limited color palette and highlighted only essential elements, avoiding unnecessary visual saturation.

Establishing the Visual Design

My goal was to create a Web Admin with a focus on simplicity and clarity so that users could clearly understand their options. I chose a color palette that included white and gray for backgrounds and the navigation bar, while the brand's green was reserved for primary actions such as "New user", "New blog", or "New notification". This color choice contributed to a more intuitive and accessible user experience.

High Fidelity Prototype

With the idea firmly established and client approval, I proceeded to create a high-fidelity prototype, achieving the following objectives:
  • I implement a navigation bar that highlighted the three available sections for navigation.
  • I organize information mainly in the form of tables, clearly emphasizing available actions.
  • I incorporate modals that allowed for efficient editing and creation of new notifications and users.
  • I use the brand's primary color to highlight buttons and important elements in the interface.

Conclusions and Learnings

Designing for an audience that is not tech-savvy presents additional challenges, as it's crucial to understand their working methods and represent information clearly and accessibly in a Web Admin. This task requires specific skills to effectively facilitate their work. My approach was centered on understanding the needs of this audience and presenting information intuitively to simplify their tasks.
